About Isro journy

About ISRO - Indian Space Research Organisation The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the space agency of the Government of India, headquartered in Bengaluru, Karnataka. Established on August 15, 1969, ISRO was founded with the vision to harness space technology for national development and to enhance India's position in the global space community.

The Story of ISRO's Founding ISRO's journey began with the vision of India's first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru, who believed that space exploration could be a critical tool for national progress. The foundation of ISRO was laid by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, widely regarded as the father of India's space program. Dr. Sarabhai, an eminent scientist, was instrumental in establishing India's space research and development capabilities. His vision was to use space technology for improving the lives of millions of people in India, especially in areas such as agriculture, telecommunications, and education.

Founder Dr. Vikram Sarabhai

Development of the PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle) and GSLV (Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle). Successful launch of Cartosat, INSAT, RISAT, and GSAT series satellites for communication, weather forecasting, and earth observation. Chandrayaan-2 mission to explore the Moon's south pole, and the upcoming Gaganyaan mission to send Indian astronauts to space. Dr. Vikram Sarabhai - The Visionary Founder Dr. Vikram Sarabhai was not just a scientist but a visionary who recognized the potential of space technology to bring about significant advancements in India’s development. His pioneering work laid the foundation for what would become one of the world’s most prominent space organizations. Dr. Sarabhai was instrumental in the establishment of the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR) in 1962, which later evolved into ISRO. His legacy continues to inspire future generations of scientists and engineers.

Future of Isro

The Future of ISRO Today, ISRO is one of the world’s most successful and cost-effective space agencies. With a focus on space exploration, satellite communication, earth observation, and space science, ISRO continues to push the boundaries of scientific discovery. The agency has ambitious plans for the future, including further lunar missions, Mars exploration, and the Gaganyaan mission, which will send Indian astronauts into space. As ISRO looks ahead, it continues to remain committed to its mission: "To use space technology for national development while pursuing space science research & planetary exploration."

Our Successful Missions

Chandrayaan-3: India’s Historic Lunar Mission

Chandrayaan-3 is the third lunar exploration mission by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). It marked a monumental achievement for India by successfully landing near the Moon’s south pole, making India the first country to do so and the fourth overall to achieve a lunar soft landing.

Gaganyaan: India’s Human Spaceflight Program

Gaganyaan is ISRO’s ambitious human spaceflight program aimed at sending Indian astronauts, or Vyomanauts, to space. It will make India the fourth country to independently conduct human space missions, following the US, Russia, and China.

Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) (2013)

Introduction: The Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), also known as Mangalyaan, is India's first interplanetary mission, launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) on November 5, 2013. It was a significant milestone for India, making it the first Asian nation to reach Martian orbit and the first nation globally to succeed in its first attempt.
