Chandrayaan-3

is India's third lunar mission, launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) on July 14, 2023. It aimed to demonstrate ISRO's capability to perform soft landings on the Moon, following the partial success of Chandrayaan-2. The mission’s primary goal was to achieve a successful landing near the Moon's south pole, a region of great scientific interest due to its potential water ice deposits. Chandrayaan-3 successfully landed on the Moon on August 23, 2023, making India the first country to land near the Moon’s south pole and enhancing India's position in global space exploration.
